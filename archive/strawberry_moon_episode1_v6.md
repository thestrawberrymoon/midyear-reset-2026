## 🌿 WHAT IS A STRAWBERRY MOON?

I want to tell you where the name comes from.

I was reading this book —

📸 **Book cover — Braiding Sweetgrass**

— *Braiding Sweetgrass* by Robin Wall Kimmerer. She talks about how Indigenous people named the full moons not after planets or gods — but after what was *happening on earth* at that very moment.

So the Strawberry Moon is simply —

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style:**
*"The moon that rises when the strawberries are ripe."*

Not grand. Not abstract. Just — this is the season. This is what the earth is doing right now.

That's what I want this channel to be. A record of what is happening, in this season of my life.

🎵 **Music softens slightly**

## 📖 HOW THIS ACTUALLY BEGAN

I didn't plan this channel. Not really.

Last July, I bought a microphone.

📸 **A photo of your mic — just the object, close and warm**

I didn't have a channel. I didn't have a plan. I just — bought it. Something in me said *not yet, but soon.*

As if buying the mic itself counted as creative progress.

And then I kept living.

The rest of 2025 was full of books, films, quotes I couldn't stop writing down. Notes piling up. Things I was slowly collecting — without knowing I was collecting them.

📸 **A photo of you reading — curled up, window light**

And then in January — I started a Substack. My first post was about perfectionism. How it had kept me from starting things I deeply wanted to start.

📸 **Screenshot — The Ordinary Moon Substack homepage**

📸 **Screenshot — your perfectionism article open**

And somewhere in that article I quoted Anne Lamott —

📸 **Book cover — Bird by Bird**

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style:**
*"Perfectionism is the voice of the oppressor. It will keep you cramped and insane your whole life, and it is the main obstacle between you and a shitty first draft."*
*— Anne Lamott, Bird by Bird*

In February — I finally started journaling. My sister had gifted me this diary and I'd been holding it, waiting to feel ready.

📸 **A photo of your Victorian diary — just the cover**

And then one day I just — opened it. And wrote.

💬 **Quote card — Moss style:**
*"An experience is not fully lived, until it is written."*
*— Annie Ernaux*

And now it's May 2026. The mic has been sitting on my desk for almost a year.

*(soft smile)*

I think it's waited long enough.

🎵 **Music shifts — a little warmer — Sarah Li energy**

## 🍓 THE AUDACITY TO BEGIN

There's this article I read recently —

📸 **Screenshot — Erifili's article headline**

— about something called **shadow artists.**

People who have always had creative dreams but keep themselves just at the edges. You want to write so you work in publishing. You want to make things but you just — watch other people make things instead. You're close. But never quite *there.*

Which is a very sophisticated way of saying: deeply attached to the idea of creating, mildly terrified of actually doing it.

💬 **Quote card — Midnight style:**
*"The belief has to come before the evidence."*

What stops most people isn't talent. It's waiting for proof before believing in yourself. But the proof only comes *after* you begin.

*(quietly)*

I think I've been a shadow artist for a long time. Writing privately. Reading deeply. Collecting all these thoughts with nowhere to put them.

The mic, the Substack, the journal, the notes — it was all pointing here.

This channel is where it lands.

🎵 **Music builds — cinematic, tender — Wild Geese / Rebekah Joy**

## 🌙 WHAT THIS CHANNEL IS

So — what is Strawberry Moon?

Long form essays. Deep research. Books I'm reading, one at a time. Ideas I can't stop turning over. The kind of thinking that doesn't fit in a caption or a thread — the kind that needs room to breathe.

📸 **A photo of you reading or at your desk — warm and present**

I'm not waiting for better lighting or a better plan.

This is my shitty first draft.

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style, the callback:**
*"Almost all good writing begins with terrible first efforts."*
*— Anne Lamott, Bird by Bird*

And that's exactly the point.
