## 🌿 WHAT IS A STRAWBERRY MOON?

I want to start by telling you where the name comes from.

I was reading this book —

📸 **Book cover — Braiding Sweetgrass**
💡 *Just the cover image, centred or beside you. Hold for 3–4 seconds*

— called *Braiding Sweetgrass* by Robin Wall Kimmerer. She's a botanist and one of the most beautiful writers I've ever read.

And she talks about how Indigenous people named the full moons not after planets or gods — but after what was *happening on earth* at that very moment.

So the Strawberry Moon is simply —

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style:**
*"The moon that rises when the strawberries are ripe."*

It's not grand. It's not abstract. It's just — this is the season. This is what the earth is doing right now.

And I thought — that is exactly what I want this channel to be.

A live record of what is happening. In this season of my life.

Not polished. Not perfectly planned. Just honest, and present, and real.

## 📖 HOW THIS ACTUALLY BEGAN

Here's the thing about this channel.

I didn't plan it. Not really.

It started — like most true things do — quietly. With one small step that didn't look like a step at all.

Last July, I bought a microphone.

📸 **A photo of your mic — just the object, close and warm**
💡 *Simple cut to this. Hold 2–3 seconds. Back to you.*

I didn't have a channel. I didn't have a plan. I just — bought it. Something in me said *not yet, but soon.*

As if buying the mic itself counted as creative progress.

And then I kept living.

The rest of 2025 was full of books, and films, and quotes I couldn't stop writing down. Notes that piled up. Things I was slowly collecting — without knowing I was collecting them.

📸 **A photo of you reading — curled up, window light, anything cosy**
💡 *Or a photo of your notes / highlights / annotated pages*

And then in January this year — I started a Substack.

📸 **Screenshot — The Ordinary Moon Substack homepage**
💡 *Full screen for 3 seconds — let the name land*

My first post was about perfectionism. How it had kept me from starting things I deeply wanted to start.

📸 **Screenshot — your perfectionism article open**
💡 *Can show just the headline and first paragraph*

And somewhere inside that article I quoted Anne Lamott —

📸 **Book cover — Bird by Bird**

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style:**
*"Perfectionism is the voice of the oppressor. It will keep you cramped and insane your whole life, and it is the main obstacle between you and a shitty first draft."*
*— Anne Lamott, Bird by Bird*

I wrote that. And kept going.

In February — I finally started journaling. Really journaling. About my life, my days, my thoughts.

📸 **A photo of your Victorian diary — just the cover, warm and beautiful**

And then one day I just — opened it. And wrote.

*— Annie Ernaux*

📸 **Screenshot — your journaling article open**

## 🍓 THE AUDACITY TO BEGIN

There's this article I read recently —

📸 **Screenshot — Erifili's article headline**
💡 *Just the title and her name visible — hold 3 seconds*

— about something called **shadow artists.**

People who have always had creative dreams but keep themselves just at the edges. You want to write so you work in publishing. You want to make things but you just — watch other people make things instead. You're close. But never quite *there.*

Which is a very sophisticated way of saying: deeply attached to the idea of creating, mildly terrified of actually doing it.

💬 **Quote card — Midnight style:**
*"The belief has to come before the evidence."*

And she says — what stops most people isn't talent. It's waiting for proof before believing in yourself. But the proof only comes *after* you begin.

No belief → you never start → you never find out.

*(quietly)*

I think I've been a shadow artist for a long time.

Writing privately. Reading deeply. Collecting all these thoughts with nowhere to put them.

And looking back now — the mic, the Substack, the journal, the notes — it was all pointing here.

This channel is where it lands.

🎵 **Music builds — cinematic, tender — Wild Geese / Rebekah Joy**

## 🌙 WHAT THIS CHANNEL IS

So — what is Strawberry Moon?

It's a live journal. With a camera.

Long form essays. Deep research. Books I'm reading, one at a time. Ideas I can't stop turning over. The kind of thinking that doesn't fit in a caption or a thread — the kind that needs room to breathe.

📸 **A photo of you reading or at your desk — warm and present**
💡 *Fade in gently alongside your voice*

📸 **Screenshot — The Ordinary Moon Substack, or Magic in the Ordinary channel**
💡 *Quick flash — just showing your little universe exists*

This channel is as much a memoir as it is a show. Part of it is for you — but part of it is for me too. A record of how I think, what I'm obsessed with, what I'm figuring out. Something I can look back on and say: this is who I was, in this particular season.

It's cosy but it's also curious. Personal but also educational. It's the Strawberry Moon — whatever is ripe and real in this particular season.

I'm not waiting for better lighting or a better plan.

This is my shitty first draft.

💬 **Quote card — Parchment style, the callback:**
*"Almost all good writing begins with terrible first efforts."*
*— Anne Lamott, Bird by Bird*

And that's exactly the point.
